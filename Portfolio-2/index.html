UE4 Japanese temple Environment - first attempt

Currently, I am working on a Japanese themed environment. This is a personal project. I received some feedback telling me to improve the contrast between the close-mid-far and the color of some assets and to add elements such as birds and falling leaves which I am planning to include soon.. This is only one side of the environment, for the other side, I am planning to make a Japanese garden that you see mostly at Asian temples. Planning the lighting for this scene took me a lot of time and tests, and I kept checking references and concepts. I wanted to achieve an afternoon golden hour lighting with warm colors and volumetric lighting interacting with the trees. I already got that mostly but there is still tweaking involved..

Neon street: 2nd Year Creative Project - First UE4 environment

These are some screenshots that I thought I'd share of my second-year project called NeonStreet (set in a Chinese setting). I was in charge of building and texturing all the architecture modularly, along with creating the landscape including the foliage, and materials, along with some street assets. I was responsible for the level design, layout, and lighting for the project. This was my first time building a 3D environment. Through the production, I have learned four new programs: Side FX Houdini, Substance Painter, Substance Designer, and UE4. I am very happy how it turned out for my first time making an environment.

ABOUTME

JASMIN KARIĆ (19)
3D GAME ARTIST

My name is Jasmin Karic and I’m a 3D Artist student from The Netherlands. My work includes 3D modeling and texturing. I mostly do environment design. I am passionate about 3D Art because I love seeing ideas become reality. The question “did I use my full potential or not?” encourages me every day.

I always go above and beyond to make my art stand out. My goal is to convey emotion in my art. Besides that, I am always curious about mostly everything, especially things that are new to me. I have worked in teams, that’s how I know what I can expect and adjust to various teams. Feel free to contact me at any time. I'm open to receive feeback!
